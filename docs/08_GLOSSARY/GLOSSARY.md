# Purpose

This glossary defines the official terminology used throughout ConstructIQ.

The purpose of this document is to ensure that developers, designers, stakeholders and users use the same vocabulary when discussing the platform.

Each term has a single official definition.

---

# General Principles

- Every business concept has one official name.
- Terms should be used consistently across documentation, database, API and UI.
- Avoid synonyms when referring to official entities.

---

# A

## Activity

A task performed on the construction site during a Working Day.

Examples:

- Excavation
- Concrete Pouring
- Bricklaying
- Painting

---

## Approval

The process through which a business record becomes officially accepted and available for calculations and reporting.

---

## Audit Trail

The complete history of changes made to a business record.

---

# B

## Budget

The planned financial allocation for a Project or Stage.

---

## Business Health

A company-level performance indicator that combines financial, operational and project metrics into a single health score.

---

## Business Rule

An official rule that defines how ConstructIQ behaves.

Business Rules are documented before implementation.

---

# C

## Client

The customer for whom the construction project is executed.

---

## Company

The organization using ConstructIQ to manage construction operations.

---

## Cost Calculation

The automatic process of calculating project costs based on approved data.

---

# D

## Dashboard

A visual summary of project or business information.

---

## Digital Twin

The digital representation of a real construction project.

Every important on-site activity should have a corresponding digital record inside ConstructIQ.

---

## Document

Any uploaded file related to a project.

Examples:

- Contract
- Drawing
- Certificate
- Delivery Note
- Inspection Report

---

# E

## Equipment

Construction machinery or tools used during project execution.

---

## Equipment Usage

The recorded operating time of equipment used during a Working Day.

---

## Extra Work

Additional work performed outside the original contract scope.

Extra Works have:

- Internal Cost
- Client Price
- Margin

---

# F

## Financial Engine

The module responsible for automatic cost calculations, budget updates and profitability analysis.

---

# I

## Invoice

A supplier invoice uploaded to ConstructIQ.

Approved invoices automatically update project costs.

---

# M

## Margin

The financial difference between:

Client Price

and

Internal Cost.

---

## Master Data

Reusable reference data shared across projects.

Examples:

- Workers
- Suppliers
- Materials
- Equipment
- Clients

---

## Material

A construction material used during project execution.

Examples:

- Concrete
- Steel
- Brick
- Cement

---

# P

## Permission

A specific capability assigned to a user through Role-Based Access Control.

Example:

invoice.approve

---

## Project

A construction contract managed within ConstructIQ.

---

## Project Health

A performance indicator showing the current operational and financial status of a single project.

---

# R

## Report

A document generated from approved project data.

Examples:

- Daily Report
- Stage Report
- Financial Report
- Client Report

---

## Role

A collection of permissions assigned to a user.

Examples:

- Owner
- Project Manager
- Site Engineer
- Accountant
- Client

---

# S

## Single Source of Truth

The principle that information is recorded once and reused everywhere.

---

## Stage

A major construction phase within a Project.

Examples:

- Foundation
- Structure
- Roof
- Finishing

---

## Supplier

A company providing materials, equipment or services.

---

# W

## Working Day

The official daily record created by the Site Engineer.

A Working Day may include:

- Attendance
- Activities
- Photos
- Documents
- Notes
- Expenses
- Equipment Usage

Approved Working Days contribute to project progress and cost calculations.

---

## Worker

A person performing construction work on-site.

Worker rates remain confidential.

---

## Workforce

The collection of workers assigned to a project or Working Day.

---

# Versioning

## Version

A new approved revision of a business record.

Approved records are never overwritten.

Corrections create new versions.

---

# Principles

ConstructIQ terminology follows:

- One Concept → One Name
- Documentation First
- Single Source of Truth
- Consistent Naming
- Clear Definitions
